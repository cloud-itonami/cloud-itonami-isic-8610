Hospital activities

ISIC 8610 — Governed inpatient care with independent clinical oversight

The problem

LLMs excel at drafting admission records and checking observation windows, but they have no jurisdiction hospital-institution license, no authority to administer treatment, and no way to verify if a clinician's license is current. Letting one act alone creates patient safety risk and liability.

An independent, spec-cited governor is not optional—it's the compliance layer you shouldn't have to build from scratch.

Open source & forkable

Our answer: HospitalOps-LLM ⊣ Clinical Oversight Governor

The LLM proposes clinical actions. An independent Governor validates every decision against jurisdiction-specific regulations and clinician credentials. Humans always approve treatment and discharge—hard holds on fabricated citations, stale licenses, and insufficient evidence are never overridden.

This is not just code: it's a complete open business model—architecture, trust controls, and an operator playbook—so any licensed hospital can fork, deploy, run, and extend it without rebuilding compliance from scratch.

What this covers

  • Admission intake: Patient registration and initial triage
  • Jurisdiction assessment: Compliance with hospital-institution licensing requirements (spec-cited per jurisdiction)
  • Credential screening: Verification that treating clinician licenses are current
  • Treatment administration: LLM-drafted proposals; human sign-off always required
  • Discharge authorization: Post-procedure observation verified; human approval always required
  • Immutable audit ledger: Every intake, assessment, screening, and actuation decision logged

What this is not

This actor does not hold or grant hospital licenses (operators supply their own jurisdiction-specific license). It does not model a full clinical-decision-support engine—no procedure-specific recovery protocols, no readmission-risk scoring, no ward-care workflows.

It is an honest, governed execution scaffold for inpatient intake through discharge authorization, nothing more. Whoever deploys and operates it supplies the clinical expertise, hospital-information-system integration, and bears the jurisdiction's liability.

Why independent governance matters

  • No treatment is administered and no discharge authorized without human clinician sign-off
  • Fabricated jurisdiction citations, incomplete clinical evidence, insufficient observation periods, and stale clinician licenses each force a hard hold—never an override
  • Double administration and double discharge guards prevent repeated actions on the same admission
  • Audit ledger captures every decision; patient health data stays outside Git
